Our Story

Where Time
Begins

In the winter of 1847, a young watchmaker named Édouard Aurum opened a modest atelier in Geneva's old town. His philosophy was simple yet revolutionary: never compromise on excellence, regardless of cost or time.

That first year, Édouard completed just twelve timepieces. Each took him nearly a month to perfect. Word spread among European nobility about the watchmaker who refused to rush his work. By 1860, Maison Aurum held royal warrants from three European courts.

Today, six generations later, we remain faithful to Édouard's vision. Every Aurum timepiece is still assembled by a single master watchmaker. We produce fewer than 3,000 watches annually — not because we cannot make more, but because we will not.

Time, after all, should never be rushed.

Édouard Aurum III
President & Master Watchmaker
Historical Milestones

Our Journey

1847

Foundation in Geneva

Édouard Aurum establishes his atelier in Rue du Rhône, creating twelve extraordinary timepieces in the first year.

1862

Royal Recognition

Appointed official watchmaker to the Royal Court of Sweden. The King's personal timepiece remains in the Swedish Royal Collection.

1891

The Perpetual Innovation

Invention of the Aurum Perpetual Calendar mechanism, requiring adjustment only once every 400 years.

1923

Golden Age of Aviation

Creation of the first Aviateur chronograph, worn by pioneering pilots crossing the Atlantic.

1955

Depth Innovation

Launch of the Nautique collection, waterproof to 300 meters — a technical marvel of its era.

1984

Return to Independence

The Aurum family reacquires 100% ownership, ensuring the maison's independence for future generations.

2010

New Manufacture

Opening of our state-of-the-art manufacture in Plan-les-Ouates, bringing all production in-house.

2024

Smart Luxury Era

Introduction of the ConnectX collection, merging Swiss mechanical excellence with intelligent technology.

The Art of Time

Forged in
Perfection

Each Aurum timepiece is assembled by a single master watchmaker over six months. More than 400 individual components — many invisible to the naked eye — work in concert to achieve absolute precision.

Single Watchmaker Philosophy

Unlike mass producers who divide assembly across specialists, each Aurum watch is crafted start-to-finish by one master watchmaker. Their signature appears inside every case back.

In-House Movements

Every component — from the smallest screw to the main plate — is designed, manufactured, and finished within our Geneva manufacture. We rely on no external suppliers.

Hand Finishing

Beveling, polishing, and decoration are performed entirely by hand. Some finishing operations take days to perfect on a single component that measures mere millimeters.

Inside Our World

The Atelier

Our manufacture in Plan-les-Ouates brings together 187 master craftspeople under one roof. From conception to completion, every Aurum watch is born here.

Movement Assembly

Where 400+ components become a single beating heart. Each movement is assembled under magnification by master watchmakers with decades of experience.

Gold Foundry

Our in-house foundry produces the 18K gold alloys used in every Aurum case. We control the color, hardness, and purity to exacting standards.

Hand Finishing

Every surface receives meticulous hand finishing. Beveling, polishing, and Geneva stripes are applied with techniques unchanged since 1847.

Quality Control

Every watch undergoes 15 days of testing in our chronometry lab. Temperature, position, and magnetic resistance are verified against Swiss chronometer standards.

Dial Manufacturing

Each dial is hand-crafted using techniques perfected over generations. Guillochage, enameling, and gem-setting happen in our dedicated dial atelier.

Strap Crafting

Our leather straps use hides from renowned French and Italian tanneries. Each is hand-stitched using traditional saddle-stitch technique.
